Focal Points — what needs attention right now
Not organized by metric category — ranked by urgency and business impact. Mix of critical risks and notable strengths worth replicating.
🔴 Critical
CXI Salary WES collapsed to 7 — steepest single-quarter drop in the entire dataset
−26 pts vs Q126 (33→7), measured against P's own benchmark trajectory (the largest, most stable Salary org). This is an acute, time-boxed event — not a slow trend — which means it is fixable fast if addressed now. Root cause confirmed in verbatims: partial THQ implementation + constantly shifting AI tool directives ("sometimes we're told to use it, then not to, then to switch tools"). P's own Salary comments independently surface the same THQ/AI-directive theme, confirming this is a company-wide irritant that becomes critical specifically where it compounds with other stressors. Sits specifically at the OM management layer (CXI S Mgr Feedback also down to 75%, −5 pts vs the company benchmark of 80%; CXI S Belonging also dips to 76%). Action this cycle: stabilize the THQ rollout and freeze AI tool-switching directives for one quarter; this single fix likely resolves WES, Mgr Feedback, and Belonging simultaneously.
🔴 Critical
LABS Salary Career Growth at 40% — lowest score anywhere in the folder, and the only org below the Company figure
−17 pts vs the company benchmark (57%) — LABS S is the only Salary org to fall below the company-wide Career Growth figure (P sits comfortably above at 67%). Verbatim is explicit and damaging: "opportunities are only for customer-service-oriented profiles, not for professionals." This is a small population (N=5) but the signal is structural, not anecdotal — cross-border HR friction is blocking formal growth paths for an entire job family. LABS Salary Belonging (64%) is also the lowest in the folder, suggesting the same structural uncertainty is affecting multiple dimensions at once. If left unaddressed, this becomes a retention risk the moment the LOB scales. Action: validate with 1:1s this cycle, then formalize a growth path for non-customer-service roles before LABS headcount grows further.
🟠 High
Career Growth is the universal floor — Company itself sits at 57%, the lowest dimension across the entire dataset
Career Growth is the lowest figure for Company across every metric tracked (57%), and MXO H ties it almost exactly at 58% — flat for 5+ consecutive quarters with no formal career ladder or published D2E criteria.
